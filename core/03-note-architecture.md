# Chapter 3 — Designing the Thinking Layer

If VIRON is a separation model, Apple Notes becomes the thinking layer.

This is where:

- Outcomes are defined.
- Scope is clarified.
- Meetings are documented.
- Ideas are captured.
- Knowledge is distilled.

Notes is not your task manager.
It is not your calendar.
It is not your execution engine.

It is your context engine.

This chapter translates philosophy into structure.

---

## The Principle of Containers

Cognitive overload does not come from too much information.

It comes from information without containers.

When everything lives in a single, undifferentiated list of notes, your brain must re-classify each note every time you scan the sidebar.

Projects mix with ideas.
Ideas mix with reference.
Reference mixes with completed work.

The result is friction.

VIRON solves this with intentional container design.

---

## The Core Folder Structure

Create the following folders:

00 – Dashboard  
01 – Projects  
02 – Ideas  
03 – Library  
04 – Archive  
99 – Templates  

Numeric prefixes are not aesthetic.
They enforce order and stability.

## Folder Roles (And Why They Exist)

### 00 – Dashboard

The Dashboard is your control surface.

It contains:

- A single master note
- Active project links
- Weekly focus theme
- High-level priorities

It is not a dumping ground.

It is a curated surface.

---

### 01 – Projects

Projects are defined by one rule:

A project has a clear outcome.

Not a theme.
Not an area.
Not a vague aspiration.

A project ends.

If it does not end, it is not a project.

Inside this folder:
- One note per active project
- Maximum five at a time

If a sixth project appears, one must move to Archive.

Constraint preserves clarity.

### 02 – Ideas

Ideas are raw.

They are not organized.
They are not evaluated.
They are not filtered.

They are captured.

This folder should contain:

- One central note called “Idea Personal”
- Or individual idea notes (if preferred)

The key principle:

Ideas are temporary.

They are either:
- Promoted to Projects
- Distilled into Library
- Deleted

If your Ideas folder grows endlessly, your Weekly Review is failing.

### 03 – Library

The Library replaces the traditional “Resources” concept.

Resources often become storage.

Library is different.

Library contains:

- Distilled principles
- Reusable frameworks
- Checklists
- Lessons learned
- Evergreen insights

Each Library note should follow one rule:

One idea per note.

Atomic.
Clear.
Written in your own language.

### 04 – Archive

Archive is a graveyard, not a workspace.

When a project ends:
- Move the entire note to Archive.
- Do not delete it.
- Do not keep it in Projects “just in case.”

Archive reduces noise without destroying history.

A clean Projects folder reduces anxiety.

---

### 99 – Templates

Apple Notes does not support native templates.

So VIRON simulates them.

Inside 99 – Templates create:

- Project Template
- Idea Template
- Weekly Review Template
- Meeting Template

Templates prevent blank-page friction.

They reduce thinking overhead.

They enforce consistency.

## Tags and Smart Folders (Optional, Not Primary)

Tags are not structure.

Folders are structure.

Tags are overlays.

Use tags for:

- #idea
- #meeting
- #review
- #strategy
- #decision
- #task (contextual mirror only)

Avoid tag explosion.

If you need 30 tags, your containers are unclear.

## How This Prevents Cognitive Leakage

Without structure:

You open Notes and see 80 mixed entries.

With structure:

You open Projects and see only active commitments.

You open Ideas and see raw thought.

You open Library and see distilled knowledge.

Each space has psychological clarity.

Your brain no longer scans irrelevant material while searching for context.

That reduction of scanning is a reduction of stress.

---

## The Stability Test

Ask yourself:

If I stopped maintaining this system for 30 days, would it collapse?

If the answer is yes, you have over-engineered it.

The Notes architecture must be:

- Minimal
- Durable
- Constrained
- Easy to reset

That is why VIRON uses five core folders.

Nothing more.

- Projects
