Reid Archer is a technologist and systems thinker focused on building practical frameworks for organizing knowledge and executing complex work.

His work explores the intersection of personal knowledge management, software automation, and long-term intellectual productivity. Through experimentation with tools such as Apple Notes, Reminders, and Shortcuts, he has developed systems designed to remain stable over long periods of use.

The VIRON Method represents the synthesis of these ideas: a layered architecture for capturing information, structuring thinking, executing projects, and allowing knowledge to compound over time.

Reid lives and works in the United States.
